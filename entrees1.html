Kung Pao Chicken Recipe

Brief Background

Kung Pao Chicken is a classic Chinese dish known for its bold flavors, featuring tender chicken stir-fried with peanuts, vegetables, and a spicy, savory sauce. It's a favorite in Chinese cuisine, combining heat, sweetness, and umami.

Ingredients

For the Chicken
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, diced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or dry sherry
For the Sauce
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water
For Stir-Frying
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 6-8 dried red chilies
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup diced bell peppers (red and green)
  • 1/2 cup diced zucchini (optional)
  • 3 green onions, chopped

Instructions

Marinate the Chicken:
  1. In a bowl, combine diced chicken with soy sauce, cornstarch, and rice wine. Mix well and set aside for 15-20 minutes.
Prepare the Sauce:
  1. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, sugar, cornstarch, and water. Set aside.
Stir-Fry the Dish:
  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil. Add dried red chilies, garlic, and ginger, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add bell peppers and zucchini, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until slightly tender.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet, then pour in the sauce. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken and vegetables.
  6. Toss in the roasted peanuts and chopped green onions, and cook for an additional minute.
Serve:
  1. Serve hot with steamed rice or fried rice on the side.
Preparation Time
  • 20 minutes
Cooking Time
  • 15 minutes
Servings
  • 4 servings
Notes
  • You can adjust the number of dried red chilies according to your preferred spice level.
  • For added flavor, you can sprinkle a few drops of sesame oil before serving.
Nutritional Information (Per Serving)
  • Calories: 420
  • Protein: 30g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Carbohydrates: 25g

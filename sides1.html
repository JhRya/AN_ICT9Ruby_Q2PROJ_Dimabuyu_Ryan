Chow Mein Recipe

Brief Background

Chow Mein is a classic Chinese dish featuring stir-fried noodles with vegetables and a savory sauce. It’s a popular side dish known for its rich flavors and satisfying texture.

Ingredients

For the Chow Mein
  • 8 oz chow mein noodles (or thin egg noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, sliced
For the Sauce
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

Prepare the Noodles:
  1. Cook the chow mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
Stir-Fry the Vegetables:
  1. In a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat, heat the vegetable oil.
  2. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry for 2 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the shredded cabbage, carrots, and bean sprouts. Stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
Add the Noodles and Sauce:
  1. Add the cooked noodles to the skillet with the vegetables.
  2. Pour the sauce over the noodles and toss everything together until well combined.
  3. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently.
Serve:
  1. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve hot.
Preparation Time
  • 10 minutes
Cooking Time
  • 15 minutes
Servings
  • 4 servings
Notes
  • You can add protein such as chicken, beef, or shrimp to make this a complete meal.
Nutritional Information (Per Serving)
  • Calories: 350
  • Protein: 8g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 45g
